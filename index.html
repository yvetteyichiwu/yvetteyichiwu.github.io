Hi! I'm Yvette (she/they), a phD student in linguistics at Harvard University, and parent to a small black cat named Luna 🐈‍⬛.

My research interests include:

  • morphology and syntax: agreement and case, adverbs, voice systems
  • language variation and contact: style and indexicality, speaker attitudes
  • history of Austronesian languages: morphosyntactic change, subgrouping

I also work with the Tgdaya Seediq community in Alang Gluban, Taiwan, with a focus on incorporating nature and art into language and cultural revitalization.

I grew up in Taiwan and California, although my family are from Singapore and Hong Kong. I studied linguistics and food systems as an undergrad at UC Berkeley, and between that and graduate school I was brewing tea, folding dumplings, and working on a permaculture farm in Taiwan.

You can email me at yichiwu@g.harvard.edu.
